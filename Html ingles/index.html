Simple past

The simple past expresses an action in the past taking place once, never, several times. It can also be used for actions taking place one after another or in the middle of another action. For example:

Infinitive

Past simple
Afirmative I went to the park
Negative I didn't eat a sandwich
Question Did you go to the park?

For irregular verbs, use the past form (see list of irregular verbs, 2nd column). For regular verbs, just add “ed”.


Past perfect

The past perfect simple expresses an action taking place before a certain time in the past. For example:

Infinitive

Past perfect
Afirmative she had left my dog alone
Negative I hadn't eaten yet.
Question Had i spoken?

For irregular verbs, use the past participle form (see list of irregular verbs, 3rd column). For regular verbs, just add “ed”.
